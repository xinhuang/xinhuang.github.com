DMCA the Ad Blocking

2017-08-12

You probably are using an ad-blocking browser plugin, e.g. Adblock, Adblock Plus or uBlock. There are filter lists inside every plugin to tell which domain or URL pattern gives ad and should be blocked.

There is a funny thing: EasyList, one of the commonly used filter list, receives a DMCA request to remove a domain, functionalclam.com, so that it will not be blocked when using EasyList.

To better understand the paradox:

Seems to me we just received a DMCA letter to alter our Firewall Rules.
Malware sites issuing DMCA letters to Anti-virus and Web-protect sites (like Google) to have their domains removed from firewall blocking.
